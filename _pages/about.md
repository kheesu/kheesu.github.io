I am interested in how linguistic, cultural, and multimodal representations emerge in modern AI systems. My current research spans LLM evaluation, semantic change, multilingual NLP, and vision-language models. More specifically, I study:

- Cultural and cross-lingual evaluation of large language models
- Semantic shift and lexical change across languages and time
- The interaction between LLM pre-training and machine translation
- Linguistic information encoded in vision-language representations
- Representation learning and embeddings for language and multimodal systems

My long-term goal is to better understand what neural representations capture and how these representations can be evaluated and improved.


## Current Research

**Cultural Evaluation of LLMs** *(ARR 2026 Submission)*  
Investigating how large language models represent and respond to culturally grounded knowledge. Contributed to the construction and evaluation of multilingual cultural benchmark datasets.

**Semantic Shift and Representation Change**  
Exploring methods for measuring semantic change through contextual embeddings. Studying how lexical meaning evolves across domains and languages.

**LLM Pre-training and Machine Translation**  
Investigating how pre-training objectives and data composition influence multilingual transfer and translation performance.

**Linguistic Structure in Vision-Language Models**  
Analyzing image embeddings from vision-language models to understand what linguistic information is encoded in multimodal representations.
